\section{Normalization in GEMPIC}

\subsection{The Vlasov-Maxwell model}

Vlasov equation for the distribution function of s particle species $s$ reads in SI units
\begin{equation}\label{Vlasov}
\partial_t f_s + \bv \cdot \nabla_{\bx} f_s + \frac{q_s}{m_s} \left( \bE + \bv \times \bB \right) \cdot \nabla_{\bv} f_s = 0
\end{equation}
and Maxwell's equations
\begin{align}
\frac{\partial \bB}{\partial t} + \nabla \times \bE &= 0, \\
- \frac{\partial \bE}{\partial t} + c^2\nabla \times \bB &= \frac{1}{\varepsilon_0}\bJ, \\
\nabla \cdot \bE &= \frac{\rho}{\varepsilon_0},\\
\nabla \cdot \bB &= 0.
\end{align}

The source terms of the Maxwell's equation are given by the velocity moments of the distribution function: the charge density
\begin{align*}
\rho= \sum_s q_s \int f_s \du\bv
\end{align*}
and the current density
\begin{align*}
\bJ =\sum_s q_s\int \bv f_s \du\bv.
\end{align*}
This system needs in addition boundary conditions and initial conditions for $\mathbf{E}$, $\mathbf{B}$ and $f$.
The initial condition for $f$ involves in general Maxwellians of the form
\begin{equation}\label{eq:maxwellian}
	f_{M,s}(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{v})= \frac{n(\mathbf{x})}{(2\pi v_{th,s}^2)^{\frac 32}} e^{-\frac{|\bv|^2}{2v_{th,s}^2}},
\end{equation}
where the thermal velocity of species $s$ is related to its temperature by $v_{th,s}=\sqrt{\frac{k_B T_s}{m_s}}$, where $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant. 

The total energy of the system is given by the Hamiltonian in SI units, 
\begin{equation}\label{hamiltonian}
\mathcal{H}=\sum_s\frac{m_s}{2}\int |\bv|^2 f_s \du\bx \du\bv + \frac{\varepsilon_0}{2} \int |\bE|^2 \du\bx + \frac{1}{2\mu_0} \int |\bB|^2 \du\bx.
\end{equation}



\subsection{Dimensionless formulation}

For all physical quantities we introduce a reference scale and a corresponding dimensionless quantity.
First for the independent variables we write
$$t= \tr \tht, ~~ \bx= \xr \xht, ~~ \bv = \vr \vht, ~~ q = \qr\qht, ~~~ m = \mr\mht.$$
For the dependent variables, we define 
\begin{align*}
	&f(t,\bx,\bv) = \fr \fht(\tht, \xht, \vht),~~~
	n(t,\bx) = \nr \nht(\tht, \xht), ~~~
	\bE(t,\bx) = \Er \Eht(\tht, \xht), \\
	&\bB(t,\bx) = \Br \Bht(\tht, \xht), ~~~
	\rho(t,\bx) = \qr\nr \rhoht(\tht, \xht), ~~~
	\bJ(t,\bx) = \qr\nr\vr \Jht(\tht, \xht), \\
	&T_s(t,\bx) = \Tr\Tsht(\tht, \xht).
\end{align*} 
so that for example 
$$\partial_t n = \frac{1}{\tr}\partial_{\tht} \nht, ~~~ \nabla_\bx n =  \frac{1}{\xr}\partial_{\tht} \nht.$$
Let us know define related physical reference quantities:
\begin{equation}
	\ompr =\sqrt{\frac{\qr^2\nr}{\varepsilon_0\mr} }, ~~~ \omcr=\frac{\qr\Br}{\mr}, ~~~ \vthr = \sqrt{\frac{k_B \Tr}{\mr}}.
\end{equation}

Plugging these expressions into the Vlasov-Maxwell system, we get the dimensionless system
\begin{equation}\label{Vlasovtr}
	\partial_{\tht} \fht_s + \frac{\vr\tr}{\xr}\vht \cdot \nablaxht \fht_s + \frac{\qr\Br\tr}{\mr} \frac{\qht_s}{\mht_s} \left( \frac{\Er}{\vr\Br}\Eht + \vht \times \Bht \right) \cdot \nablavht \fht_s = 0
	\end{equation}
	and Maxwell's equations
	\begin{subequations}
	\begin{align}
	\frac{\partial \Bht}{\partial \tht} + \frac{\tr \Er}{\Br\xr}\nablaht \times \Eht &= 0 , \\
	-  \frac{\partial \bE}{\partial t} + \frac{\tr\Br}{\xr\Er} c^2\nabla \times \Bht &= \frac{\qr\nr\vr\tr}{\varepsilon_0\Er}\Jht , \\
	\nabla \cdot \bE &= \frac{\qr\nr\xr}{\varepsilon_0\Er}\rhoht,\\
	\nablaht \cdot \Bht &= 0.
	\end{align}
	\end{subequations}
	The source terms of the Maxwell's equation are given by the velocity moments of the distribution function: the charge density
	\begin{align*}
	\rhoht= \sum_s Z_s \int \fht_s \du\vht
	\end{align*}
	and the current density
	\begin{align*}
	\Jht =\sum_s Z_s\int \vht \fht_s \du\vht.
	\end{align*}
	Note that for the natural choice $\qr=e$ the electron charge, $Z_s$ is the charge state of species $s$.

Using the relation between $\fr$ and $\nr$, the dimensionless Maxwellian is 
\begin{equation}\label{eq:maxwellianD}
	\fht_{M,s}(\xht,\vht)= \frac{\nht(\xht)}{(2\pi \hat{v}_{th,s}^2)^{\frac 32}} e^{-\frac{|\bv|^2}{2\hat{v}_{th,s}^2}},
\end{equation}	

We shall now choose the following relations between our reference quantities
\begin{equation}
	\frac{\vr\tr}{\xr}=1, ~~~ \frac{\Er}{\vr\Br}=1, ~~~ \frac{\qr\Br\tr}{\mr} = \omcr\tr =1.
\end{equation} 	
From these choices it follows that
\begin{equation}
	\frac{\tr \Er}{\Br\xr} = 1, ~~~ \frac{\tr\Br}{\xr\Er} c^2 = \frac{c^2}{\vr^2}, ~~~
	\frac{\qr\nr\vr\tr}{\varepsilon_0\Er}= \frac{\qr\nr\xr}{\varepsilon_0\Er}= \frac{\ompr^2}{\omcr^2}.
\end{equation}

With these choices all the coefficients in our Vlasov-Maxwell system are set to one except the two scaling parameters
\begin{equation}
	s_v = \frac{c}{\vr}, ~~~~ s_\omega = \frac{\ompr}{\omcr}.
\end{equation}

Finally the dimensionless Vlasov-Maxwell system implemented in the code reads
\begin{equation}\label{Vlasovt}
	\partial_{\tht} \fht_s + \vht \cdot \nablaxht \fht_s +  \frac{\qht_s}{\mht_s} \left( \Eht + \vht \times \Bht \right) \cdot \nablavht \fht_s = 0
	\end{equation}
	\begin{subequations}
	\begin{align}
	\frac{\partial \Bht}{\partial \tht} + \nablaht \times \Eht &= 0 , \\
	-  \frac{\partial \Eht}{\partial t} + s_v^2\nablaht \times \Bht &= s_\omega^2\Jht , \\
	\nablaht \cdot \Eht &= s_\omega^2\rhoht,\\
	\nablaht \cdot \Bht &= 0.
	\end{align}
	\end{subequations}
	The source terms of the Maxwell's equation are given by the velocity moments of the distribution function: the charge density
	\begin{align*}
	\rhoht= \sum_s Z_s \int \fht_s \du\vht
	\end{align*}
	and the current density
	\begin{align*}
	\Jht =\sum_s Z_s\int \vht \fht_s \du\vht.
	\end{align*}

The total energy of the dimensionless system is given by the following dimensionless Hamiltonian, 
	\begin{equation}\label{hamiltoniant}
	\mathcal{H}=\sum_s\frac{\mht_s}{2}\int |\vht|^2 \fht_s \du\xht \du\vht + \frac{1}{2s_\omega^2} \int \|\Eht\|^2 \du\xht + \frac{s_v^2}{2s\omega^2} \int \|\Bht\|^2 \du\xht.
	\end{equation}

	\subsection{Code units}

A plasma is characterized by three reference parameters: density, temperature and background magnetic field (for a magnetized plasma). In addition, the charge and mass of each species in the plasma need to be given. These reference values need to be set by the user. All the other plasma quantities follow from these.

We choose the electrons as the reference particles, so that
$$\qr = e, ~~~ \mr=m_e, ~~~ \omcr=\frac{e\Br}{m<-e}, ~~~ \ompr=\frac{e^2\nr}{\epsilon_0m_e}, ~~~ \vthr= \sqrt{\frac{k_B \Tr}{m_e}} .$$

In addition, in our scaling we made the assumptions that $\tr = \omcr^{-1}$. 
Then setting the reference velocity $\vr$ completely defines our two scaling parameters $s_v$ and $s_\omega$.
We also denote by $d_e$ the electron skin depth (inertial length) and by
$\lambda_D=\vthr/\omr$ the reference Debye length.
A summary of the reference quantities for choice 1 ($\vr=c$) and choice 2 ($\vthr$) is given in table \ref{tab:refq}.

\begin{table}[htbp]
	\centering\begin{tabular}{c|c|c}
		Reference quantity & Choice 1 & Choice 2 \\ \hline
		$\nr$& arbitrary & arbitrary\\
		$\Tr$& arbitrary& arbitrary\\
		$\Br$& arbitrary& arbitrary\\
		$\qr$& e & e \\
		$\mr$& $m_e$  & $m_e$  \\
		$\tr$& $ \omcr^{-1}$ &$ \omcr^{-1}$\\
		$\vr$& $c$ &$\vthr$ \\ 
		$\xr$& $s_\omega d_e$ & $s_\omega \lambda_D$ \\

	\label{tab:refq}
\end{table}

By default the two scaling parameters are set to 1. This means on the one hand ($s_v=1$) that the reference velocity is $c$ the speed of light and on the other hand $(s_\omega=1$) that $\omega_{p,e}=\omega_{c,e}$ which imposes a dependence between $\nr$ and $\Br$.

Other values can be set in the input file by setting
\verb|epref_ep0| ($=s_\omega$) and \verb|curlB_scaling| (=$s_v$).

\subsection{Examples of plasma parameters}

A magnetized plasma is characterized by its density $n$, its temperature $T$ and the magnitude of its external magnetic field $B$. These beings known as well as the particle mass and charges, the following parameters can be computed  

\begin{itemize}
 \item Thermal velocity $v_{th}= \sqrt{\frac{k_B T}{m}}$ 
 \item Plasma frequency $\omega_p = \frac{q^2 n}{\epsilon_0 m}$
 \item Cyclotron frequency  $\omega_c = \frac{q B}{m}$
 \item thermal gyroradius $\rho_L = \frac{v_{th}}{\omega_c}$
 \item inertial length $d=\frac{c}{\omega_p}$
 \item Debye length $\lambda_D = \frac{v_{th}}{\omega_p}$ 
 \item Alfven velocity $v_A = \frac{B_0}{\mu_0 m_i n}$
 \item ratio thermal to magnetic energy $\beta = \frac{2\mu_0 n k_B T}{B_0^2}$
\end{itemize}

Let us give typical values for two kinds of magnetized plasmas: a magnetic fusion plasma and a ionosphere plasma, for only hydrogen ions, so that $q_i = -q_e = 1.6022\times 10^{-19} \,C$, $m_e =  9.1094 \times 10^{-31} \,kg$, $m_i=1.6726 \times 10^{-27}\,kg$.

	$v_{th}= \sqrt{k_B T / m}$.
